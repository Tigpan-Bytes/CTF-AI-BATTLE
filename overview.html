Bee Swarm Battle: Overview
CTF: Bee Swarm Battle

   Bee Swarm Battle is similar to the Real Time Strategy genre. It is a multiplayer, simultaneous-turns game that takes place on a large interconnected procedurally generated grid. Many players will inhabit the grid at one time. You construct an AI that controls your Bees to destroy the enemies as efficently as possible without getting yourself killed in the process.

   Every Team will write AI's that control Bees to collect food, grow their colony and crush others. Teams may submit as many AI's as they wish, but only the latest submission will get used in the competition. Every 15mins each team's most recent AI is tested against all the other team's AI's. The team who gets first gets 50 points, second gets 40 points, and third gets 30 points

Map, Food, and Hives

  The map is always a pattern of repeating 32x32 tile areas. Where 0,0 is in the bottom left corner of the map. The number of repeating grids on the x and y axis is dependant on how many AI's are able to participate. It is also important to note that the map wraps across both axis.

  In the picture there are light green squares. These are food tiles. When walked over by a bee (the diamond shaped object), they are collect for that colony. That colony gets 2 'food value', distributed evenly between the colony's remaining hives. A hive cannot have more than 10 'food value'. At some point in the turn (explained later), for each hive, if there is no bee standing on it and if its food value is greater than 0, then decrease its food value by one and spawn a friendly bee. If a hive is stepped on by an enemy bee, that hive is destroyed. Every colony starts with 2 hives, and 10 bees (5 bees arranged around each hive in a + shape).

   The game starts with 3 food tiles in each grid, these are mirrored to the other grids for fairness. Food is spawned per turn based on the colony with the highest bee count. If the highest bee count is less than 80, than food is spawned every 8 turns. If the highest bee count is less than 140 and greater or equal to 80, than food is spawned every 16 turns. Finally if the highest bee count is less than 200 and greater or equal to 140, than food is spawned every 32 turns, and if the highest bee count is greater or equal to 200 then no food is spawned.

The Bee

  The Bee is the only thing in the game that the AI's have direct control over. Bees can be instucted to move, attack, collect food, and they can even die. They also have 6 health (explained later). While your AI may influence your hive, they act automatically, bees are the only thing controlled directly. Bees can be commanded to take 1 of 2 actions.



  Move: A move action simply moves the bee in 1 of the 4 cardinal direction (North, South, East, or West). To execute a move action you set the bees action variable to 'M ' + (Either N, S, E, or W). For example: 'M S' would move the bot one space to the north (0 on the x axis and -1 on the y axis). Bees cannot pass through other bees. If two friendly bees attempt to move into each other, no movement occurs. If bees on opposite teams collide, the one with the lower health will remain alive. If both opposing bees have the same health, they both die. Bees can collect food by moving on top of it.

  The AI Battle is also programmed to attempt to rectify any collisions. This means if there is any way all the bees can move along their chosen path without any collisions, the game will use that pattern.

  An attack action will be rejected if you attempt to move into a wall, a friendly bee, or input a direction other than North, South, East, or West.



  Attack: An attack action instructs the bee to shoot any tile in a range of 3 spaces away, regardless. In the left example the bee (B) can shoot all the x tiles, but none of the . tiles. In the right example the bee can shoot all the same x tiles, even through walls (W).

  An attack action will be rejected if it is more than 3 spaces away. However, friendly fire is allowed, use this strategically so your bees can move into enemy bees without dying.

Turn Order

  Turns occur in this order.

    1. Spawn Food.
    2. Get AI input (the AI's do_turn function).
    3. Attack actions.
    4. Kill low health bees.
    5. Move actions.
    6. Collect food.
    7. Destroy hives.
    8. Spawn new bees.

    Repeat.

Game End

   The game ends when one of two conditions are met.

   1. There is only one bot remaining.

   In this situation 1st, 2nd, and 3rd are decided in the order of death. Last AI alive is 1st last to die is 2nd, second last to die is 3rd.

   2. The turn counter reaches 750.

   In this situation 1st, 2nd, and 3rd are decided according to points. Colonies start with 60 points, get 20 for destroying and enemy hive, lose 30 for having a friendly hive destroyed, get one point for every 10 turns survived, and 1 point for every 4 food collected.

AI Loss and Termination

   An AI can lose the battle when all of their hives are destroyed. An AI can also be terminated. Being terminated means they did one of three things:

   1. Runtime error. When a bot encounters a runtime error it is immediatly terminated. This means the AI is killed and given 0 points, (their position in the order of death is uneffected, they act as if they just died). In the application this is shown as TERMINATED [Naughty].

   2. Timeout error. If your bot runs for more than 0.5 seconds it will not do any actions that turn and it will. recieve 1 'time out' if a bot gets 5 'time outs' then they are terminated with the message TERMINATED [Timeout].

   3. Security threat. This is automatically triggered if your bot attempts to import things other than the default imports, or if you try to open a file. Also if the tournament organizer deems that running your Bot is a security risk, then it will not be run and your team may be banned from participating in further Bee Swarm Battle rounds.
